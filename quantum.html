Shor's Algorithm — Why RSA Will Break

This is an educational visualiser — a simplified step-by-step animation showing how Shor's algorithm works conceptually. Real quantum execution requires quantum hardware.

RSA security relies on the fact that factoring large numbers is computationally hard. A classical computer factoring RSA-2048 would take longer than the age of the universe. Shor's algorithm uses quantum superposition and Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT) to do it in polynomial time O(log³N) — estimated 8 hours on a mature quantum computer (Gidney & Ekerå, 2021).

Grover's Algorithm — Why AES-128 and MD5 Are Weakened

Classical brute force searches N items in O(N) time. Grover's algorithm searches in O(√N) — a quadratic speedup. This halves the effective key length of symmetric encryption: AES-128 becomes effectively AES-64 — classically breakable.

Post-Quantum Cryptography — The Solution

NIST finalised these standards in August 2024. QuantumGuard recommends these exact algorithms as replacements.

🔐
CRYSTALS-Kyber
NIST FIPS 203

Replaces RSA for key exchange. Based on Module Learning With Errors (MLWE) — a mathematical problem Shor's algorithm cannot attack.

Replaces: RSA, DH, ECDH
✍️
CRYSTALS-Dilithium
NIST FIPS 204

Replaces ECDSA and RSA for digital signatures. Also based on MLWE lattice problem — secure against quantum attacks.

Replaces: ECDSA, RSA-PSS, DSA
🔒
SHA-3-256
NIST FIPS 202

Replaces MD5 and SHA-1. Uses Keccak sponge construction — fundamentally different from MD5/SHA-1. Grover's attack requires 2¹²⁸ operations — infeasible.

Replaces: MD5, SHA-1, SHA-256
